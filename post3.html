üìú Brief History of the Internet

The internet began in the 1960s as ARPANET, a U.S. military project designed to create a communication system capable of surviving a nuclear attack. In 1969, the first message was sent between two computers, marking the beginning of modern networking.

During the 1970s, TCP/IP was introduced‚Äîforming the basic rules for data transmission. The first email was also sent in 1971.

By the 1980s, the network expanded beyond the military, and the Domain Name System (DNS) was created, making websites easier to access.

The 1990s saw a revolution with the invention of the World Wide Web by Tim Berners-Lee, and the Mosaic browser, which brought the internet to the public. Search engines and e-commerce also emerged.

From 2000 onward, the rise of social media, cloud computing, and mobile internet transformed digital communication. In the 2010s, IoT became widespread.

Today, the internet continues to evolve with AI, Web3, and quantum computing, shaping the future of global connectivity.
